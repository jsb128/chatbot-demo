How to find businesses near you
Build your target list before you go anywhere
▼

Before you drive anywhere, build a list of 20 local businesses. Here's exactly how:

Google Maps method: Open Google Maps, search "electrician near me" or "plumber near me." Look for businesses with fewer than 50 reviews — these are small owner-operated businesses, not big companies. Write down their name, address, and phone number.

What to look for: Businesses where the owner is also the worker. A solo electrician or a 2-3 person shop. Not a company with 20 employees — those have receptionists already.

💡 Pro Tip Check if they have a website. If they don't — that's your best lead. No website means they definitely need both your website and chatbot package.

Your goal: Build a list of 20 businesses before you make a single visit or call. Split them into two groups — ones close enough to visit in person, and ones you'll cold call.

02
Method 1
In-Person Visits — The Highest Converting Method
👔
What to wear and when to go
First impressions matter more than you think at 18
▼

What to wear: Clean, neat, and simple. Dark jeans or chinos, a plain button-up or clean polo. No hoodies, no sneakers with holes. You don't need a suit — you need to look like someone a business owner would trust with their business.

When to go: Aim for slow hours. For tradespeople like electricians and plumbers, try early morning before 9am or call their office mid-afternoon 2–4pm. For barber shops and salons, go on a weekday morning when it's quiet.

Never go: During lunch rush, Friday afternoons, or any time the place looks packed and chaotic. You want the owner to have 3 minutes to talk, not be stressed.

⚠️ Reality Check You're 18. Some owners will dismiss you immediately because of your age. Don't take it personally — move on. The ones who give you a chance are your clients. You only need a few to make good money.
🗣️
The in-person script — word for word
Memorize this before you walk into anyone's business
▼

Walk in, ask for the owner. When you get them, say this:

Opening — 30 seconds

"Hi, I'm [your name]. I build AI assistants for local businesses — basically a smart chatbot that answers customer questions 24/7 so you never miss a lead when you're busy on a job. I built one for a local electrician and wanted to show you how it works. Do you have two minutes?"

If they say yes — pull out your phone and open your demo link. Hand them the phone and say:

The Demo Hand-Off

"Just type something a customer would ask you — like your hours, your pricing, or what areas you cover."

Let them type. Let them see it answer. Stay quiet and let the demo do the talking. Then say:

After the Demo

"I can build one of these for your business in about two days. It would know everything about your services, your pricing, your area — whatever you want. Customers can use it on your website or you can share the link anywhere. How many calls do you think you miss in a week when you're out on a job?"

✅ Why that last question works It makes them do the math themselves. If they say "probably 5 or 6 calls a week" and each call is worth $300, they just told themselves they're losing $1,500+ a week. Your $50/month suddenly sounds like nothing.
💰
How to close and talk pricing
What to say when they ask "how much does it cost?"
▼

When they ask about price, be direct and confident. Don't apologize for your price or hesitate.

Pricing Script

"It's $200 to set it up — that covers me building it, training it on all your business info, and getting it live. Then it's $50 a month after that which covers updates, maintenance, and keeping it running. Most of my clients make that back from one extra job a month that the chatbot captures."

If they want to think about it, don't pressure them. Say:

If They Need Time

"Totally fine. Here's what I'll do — let me send you the demo link so you can play with it yourself and show your wife/partner/team. What's the best number to follow up with you in a couple days?"

💡 Getting Their Number Always leave with their phone number or email. A maybe with contact info is worth way more than a maybe with nothing.

Method 2
Cold Calling — For Days You Can't Drive Out
📞
Before you call — mindset
Cold calling feels scary. Here's how to think about it
▼

Most people hang up or say no. That's normal and expected. Cold calling is a numbers game — if 1 in 10 people says yes, you just need to make 10 calls to get a client.

The right mindset: You're not begging them to buy something. You're offering to solve a real problem they have. If they hang up, they're not your customer — next one might be.

✅ Your Goal Per Call You're not trying to close a sale on the phone. Your only goal is to get them interested enough to receive your demo link via text. That's it. Close the call with a link, not a contract.
📱
The cold call script — word for word
Keep it under 60 seconds
▼

When someone picks up, say this immediately — don't pause or stumble:

Cold Call Opening

"Hi, is this the owner? — Great. My name is [name], I'll keep this super short. I build AI assistants for local [electricians/plumbers/etc] that answer customer questions automatically when you're busy on a job. I built a demo for a local electrician and wanted to see if it would be useful to you. Can I text you the link so you can try it yourself?"

If they say yes: Get their number, text them the demo link immediately while they're on the phone. Say "I just sent it — let me know what you think and we can talk more if it seems useful."

If they say they're busy:

If They're Busy

"No problem at all — what's a better time to reach you? I'll call back then."

If they say not interested: Say "No worries, thanks for your time" and hang up. Don't try to convince them. Move to the next call.

Handling Pushback
What to Say When They Object
🛡️
The 5 most common objections and how to handle them
Know these before you go — you will hear all of them
▼
"I'm not good with technology."
"That's exactly why this works for you — you don't touch it at all. I build it, I set it up, I maintain it. You just have customers getting answers automatically. You don't need to do anything." Why it works: removes their fear of having to learn something new.
"I already have a website / Facebook page."
"That's great — this would actually sit right on your existing website as a chat bubble in the corner. Your customers could ask it questions any time, even at 2am, and it would answer instantly. Does your current setup do that?" Why it works: turns their objection into a reason they need you.
"How do I know it actually works?"
"That's fair — here, try it yourself right now." [hand them your phone with the demo open] "Ask it anything a customer would ask you." Why it works: you stop talking and let the product prove itself.
"I can't afford it right now."
"I understand. The setup fee is $200 and then $50 a month — most of my clients cover that from just one extra job the chatbot captures in the first month. But if timing is off, I can check back in with you next month. What's a good way to stay in touch?" Why it works: keeps the door open without being pushy.
"You seem young — how long have you been doing this?"
"I'm 18 and I've been building these for about [X months]. Honestly the technology is so new that most people doing this are young — we grew up with it. What I can tell you is the demo works, and if it doesn't deliver value in the first month, I'll refund you. No risk on your end." Why it works: honest, confident, and removes their risk completely.

After They Say Yes
What Happens When You Land a Client
🤝
What to do immediately after they agree
The first 24 hours after a sale matter
▼

When someone says yes, here's your exact next steps:

Collect their setup fee ($200) via cash, Venmo, Zelle, or PayPal before you start any work
Send them a simple one page agreement (ask Claude to write one for you)
Ask them for: business name, phone, hours, services, pricing, service area, and any FAQs
Tell them you'll have it ready within 48 hours
Build their chatbot using their info — use Claude to format the content
Test it thoroughly before delivering it
Send them the live link and walk them through it over the phone
Set up the monthly $50 payment via Venmo or Zelle recurring
⭐
Always ask for a referral and a review
This is how you get clients 2, 3, and 4 without cold calling
▼

About a week after delivering their chatbot, follow up and ask two things:

Referral Ask

"Glad it's working well. Do you know any other local business owners who might find this useful? Even just a name — I'll reach out to them and mention you sent me."

Review Ask

"Would you mind leaving me a quick Google review? It really helps when I'm reaching out to other business owners. I can send you the link right now if that makes it easier."

✅ Why This Matters A referral from a happy client converts almost 100% of the time. You walk in and say "Mike from Mike's Plumbing sent me" and the sale is basically already made. This is how your business grows without you having to cold call forever.

Stay Organized
How to Track Your Leads
📋
Keep a simple spreadsheet of every lead
Most sales happen on the 2nd or 3rd follow up — not the first
▼

Open Google Sheets and create a simple tracker with these columns:

Business name
Owner name
Phone number
Date contacted
Status (Contacted / Demo Sent / Interested / Client / Not Interested)
Follow up date
Notes

Every person you talk to goes in this sheet. Every follow up gets scheduled. Most people say no the first time and yes the third time — you'll lose sales if you don't track this.
